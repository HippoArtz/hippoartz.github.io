Meet the Acrylic Alchemist

Welcome to HIPPO.ARTZ. This site is a novice apothecary of my experimentation with color, texture, and storytelling. I am Nita — a self-taught acrylic painter, visual storyweaver, and unapologetic blerd. My canvases explore how emotion and identity take shape through broad strokes, surreal dreamscapes, and a touch of Black Girl Magic.

Behind the Canvas

“I am a pessimist if I am not careful.” — Octavia E. Butler

Every piece I create is layered with emotion, lived experience, and a shimmer of Black Girl Magic. Bold, intentional strokes carry my reflections on the people I meet, the state of the world, and the dreams that hover just behind my eyelids. My medium is colorful acrylic paints on canvas and unfinished wood.

My creative journey has been ongoing — nourished and sustained by curiosity and grit. Every brushstroke is an experiment and a chance to grow. If there is a single theme, it’s a feeling. My experience as a melanin-rich, intelligent, unapologetic, witty woman drives my work toward struggle, independence, resilience, surreal dreamscapes, and death-positive imagery.

When I am not painting, I am a Plague Master (aka public health professional) and staff to my two cats, Fawn and Goatee. This site is both a portfolio and a playground. Whether you’re here to browse, reflect, or connect, I’m grateful you stopped by. Be well.

Current Creative Projects

Unheard Echoes

Black women worldwide are consigned to carry the burdens of the world, and we do so without complaint. Despite chains that weigh us down, crowns that slip, or tears drying on our cheeks, we endure. These pieces are the shouts of the voiceless: a hand extended to those who stumble, and a tribute to those who keep pushing regardless of the treachery of the journey or its destination.

Dreamscapes and Nightmares

Without darkness, how would we know the light? These images drag monsters and myth from the shadows. Eerie and surreal, they straddle the thin veil between nightmare and fable.

Queens

Tightly wound kinky coifs, ebony-rich curves, and the rhythm of our roots. These works explore beauty, visibility, identity, representation, and belonging. We are here.
